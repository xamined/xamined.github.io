The Announcement

July 1, 2026 — two announcements, one day

On July 1, 2026, PlayStation published two blog posts. The first announced that starting January 2028, no new PlayStation game will release on a physical disc. Every future title will be sold exclusively through the PlayStation Store as a digital download.

The second announced that the PlayStation Store on PS3 and PS Vita will shut down. In the US and UK, stores close by July 2027. In Latin America and the Middle East, closures begin as early as August 2026.

72 million views in 18 hours. The official PlayStation tweet announcing both changes received 53,000 comments, 53,000 retweets, and 50,000 likes.

The Ownership Problem

You never owned your digital games. The terms of service say so.

The PlayStation Store Terms of Service state that users purchase a license to access content, not the content itself. When Sony's agreement with a publisher expires, or when Sony decides to delist content, the license expires with it.

On June 26, 2026, five days before the disc announcement, Sony emailed thousands of UK users that 551 movies and TV shows they had paid for would be permanently removed from their libraries. Titles included John Wick, Terminator 2, Hot Fuzz, and Paddington.

Games face the same risk. Titles have been delisted from the PlayStation Store before. When the PS3 and PS Vita stores close in 2027, games exclusive to those platforms that were never released on disc will become inaccessible, the same outcome as the Wii Shop Channel closure in 2019.

85% of all PS5 software was purchased digitally in the most recent quarter (Q4 FY2025). Only 15% of purchases were physical discs. Sony cited this trend as the reason for ending disc production.
