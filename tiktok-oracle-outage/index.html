Timeline

Jan 25, 2026
Outage #1. Winter storm knocks out power at an Oracle data center. TikTok goes dark for eight days. Three days after the deal closed.
Mar 3, 2026
Outage #2. Network switch fails at Oracle's Ashburn, VA data center. Cascading power distribution fault takes out storage clusters. Twenty hours of downtime.

The Architecture Problem

Why centralization created a single point of failure.

Project Texas was TikTok's $1.5 billion data security initiative. All US user traffic routed to Oracle Cloud Infrastructure. The recommendation algorithm runs inside OCI. Oracle reviews the source code and monitors data flows.

The engineering tradeoff: ByteDance ran TikTok on a globally distributed network. If one region went down, others absorbed the load. Oracle's setup concentrates everything in one auditable location. When Ashburn goes down, TikTok goes down. No fallback.

According to Harvard Law School, the deal may have made the security problem worse. The intense oversight TikTok operated under before the sale is gone. The US still has no comprehensive privacy law, so TikTok now collects the same data with less scrutiny.

The Fallout

Censorship panic, creator exodus, and a viral copypasta.

During the January outage, users noticed political posts getting less engagement. A theory spread that Oracle was censoring content critical of the Trump administration. California Governor Newsom launched an investigation. Researchers later found no evidence of deliberate suppression. All topics saw engagement drops because the servers were broken.

Creators started downloading their archives. Meg Stalter announced she was deleting her page. Slate ran the headline: "TikTok Really Broke. We Should Just Let It Die." A viral chain message told users to "block Oracle's TikTok account" to fix the algorithm.
