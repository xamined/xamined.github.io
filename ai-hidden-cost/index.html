PJM Capacity Auction History

PJM Interconnection runs the wholesale electricity market for 13 states and DC, serving 65 million people. These are the base residual auction clearing prices that determine what utilities pay for future generating capacity.
Delivery Year Clearing Price Total Cost Change
2024/2025 $28.92/MW-day (RTO) $2.2B Baseline
2025/2026 $269.92/MW-day (RTO); BGE: $466; DOM: $444 $14.7B +833%
2026/2027 $329.17/MW-day (price cap hit) $16.1B +22%
2027/2028 $333.44/MW-day (price cap, 6,625 MW shortfall) ~$16.4B First-ever capacity shortfall
Data center attribution: PJM's Independent Market Monitor attributes 63% ($9.3B) of the 2025/2026 cost increase to data center demand. CUB (Illinois) independently estimated 70%. Across three auction cycles, data centers account for 45% ($21.3B of $47.2B) of total capacity costs. Data centers drive 94-97% of all new demand in PJM's load growth forecast.

Virginia rate disparity: Residential customers pay ~17.5 c/kWh while data centers pay ~8.5 c/kWh (2:1 disparity). The Dominion zone cleared at $444.26/MW-day, 65% higher than the rest of PJM, driven by Northern Virginia's status as the world's largest data center market. The Virginia SCC approved a new data center rate class starting January 2027.
PJM-wide average: Across ~27 million households, the average incremental capacity cost is ~$38-40/month. Long-term projection: $70/month by 2028 (Introl); $163B from 2028-2033 if the price cap expires (NRDC).
